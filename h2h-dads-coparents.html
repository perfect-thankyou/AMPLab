Why Fathers and Co-parents Matter for Family Health

Health care and research have often focused on just one parent - usually mom. But the whole family matters.

The Missing Pieces

When we look at kids' health - in doctor's offices or in research - we often only hear from moms. But think about what we're missing:

What's Been Left Out:

How fathers' and co-parents' health affects kids
What happens to YOUR health when you become a parent
What fathers and co-parents really need to stay healthy
How different families actually work

The Result: Health programs that don't fit real families. Advice that doesn't work for fathers and co-parents. Missing pieces in understanding why health problems pass from parent to child.

H2H is different. We give every mom who becomes pregnant the chance to invite fathers and co-parents - because we know you matter. Your health, your habits, and what you think are key pieces of keeping families healthy. We can't make programs that work for real families without hearing from you.

The Science Behind Why You Matter

For Biological Fathers

Your Body, Your Baby's Future

Scientists have found that a dad's health around the time of conception affects the baby in ways we're just starting to understand:

  • Your genes (obviously) - but it's more complex than we thought
  • What you eat and drink can actually change how your genes work
  • Your stress levels might affect your baby's future health
  • Even your age and weight at conception matter

But here's what we DON'T know yet: Exactly HOW MUCH this matters and WHAT we can do about it. That's where H2H comes in.

For Co-parents

(partners, grandparents, chosen family - anyone who helps raise the child)

Your Daily Life Shapes Everyone's Health

  • Kids copy what they see you do every day
  • Your food choices become family food choices
  • Your activity level sets the tone for the whole house
  • The stress or calm you bring home affects everyone

Plus: Parenting Changes YOUR Health

  • Your sleep (or lack of it)
  • Your eating habits (hello, leftover chicken nuggets)
  • Your stress levels
  • Your own heart health risks

Some people are BOTH the biological dad AND a co-parent. Some are one or the other. All perspectives are valuable.

What Makes H2H Different

We're not just collecting data - we're listening to what you think matters

Other studies might measure your blood pressure or count your steps. H2H goes deeper:

We ask you to rate what helps or gets in your way for staying healthy - from motivation to money to support
You tell us your real sources of stress - the actual stuff that affects your family
Some fathers and co-parents will share deeper thoughts in optional interviews about what would really help

A Message from Dr. Perak

"As a pediatric heart doctor, I see families every day trying their best. I see dads and co-parents who want to help but don't always know how. I see health problems passing from generation to generation, and we don't know how to stop it.

That's why I need your voice in H2H. Moms are telling us their side of the story. Now we need yours. Not what you think we want to hear - but what's really going on in your life.

Frequently Asked Questions

How did you get my contact info?
Someone in H2H invited you - likely the mom of your child or expected child. We encourage you to talk with them about which child and why they joined.
Which child is this about?
If you are a father or co-parent to more than one child, check with the mom who invited you. She'll know which child is part of H2H.
I got an invite but I'm not sure from who
For privacy, we don't share names. If you're having a baby or helping raise a child ages 0-5, ask that child's mom if she's in H2H.
Do I have to be with the mom?
No. We want to hear from all fathers and co-parents, no matter your relationship status.
What if I barely see my kid?
Your participation still matters. Your genetics (if you're the biological father) or your influence (when you are present) still affects the child.
I'm not the biological dad but I'm raising this child. Do I count?
Absolutely. Co-parents are just as important to H2H. Your daily influence might matter even more than genetics.
What if I'm both the biological father AND the co-parent?
Perfect! You might get more than one invitation and have the opportunity to do more than one 30-minute survey. Your perspective in both of these roles is valuable.
Is this legit?
Yes! H2H is run by Lurie Children's Hospital and Northwestern University, funded by the National Institutes of Health. Check out the main study page at sites.northwestern.edu/amplab/h2h
