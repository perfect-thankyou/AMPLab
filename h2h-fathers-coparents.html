What is H2H & Why Are You Needed?

H2H is learning how to build stronger hearts and better lives for families - and your voice changes everything

The Problem

Most health programs for families have big gaps. They rarely ask fathers and co-parents what matters. The result? Programs that don't work for real families.

Photo: Father and co-parent in healthcare setting with child - engaged but highlighting their voice often isn't heard

H2H is Different - We Care About YOU Too

H2H is one of the first big studies to include fathers and co-parents as equal partners, alongside moms and babies. Yes, we want to know how you affect your family's health. But we also want to know what affects YOUR health. What stresses you out? What helps you stay healthy? What gets in your way?

Your impact: Your answers will help create programs that work for dads and co-parents, not just for kids and moms.

The Science - Why Your Role Matters

Whether you are a biological father, a co-parent, or both, the H2H community needs your input.

For Biological Fathers

Your health affects your baby through:

  • Your genes (it's more complex than we thought)
  • Your lifestyle (it can change how your genes work!)
  • Your stress levels
  • Your effect on mom's health (if you're partners)

Becoming a dad can also change YOU:

  • New emotions - joy, worry, stress
  • Changes in sleep, eating, and activity
  • New responsibilities
  • Your own heart health

For Co-Parents

(partners, grandparents, other caregivers)

Your daily life shapes:

  • Kids' habits (they copy what you do)
  • Family food choices
  • How active everyone is
  • The mood of the whole house

Being a co-parent can also change YOU:

  • Your sleep (or lack of it)
  • Your eating habits and activity
  • Your stress levels
  • Your own heart health

What H2H is learning: How much all this matters and what we can do to help fathers and co-parents stay healthy.

Together, the H2H community is answering big questions:
1

When does parent health matter most for kids?

Before baby? During pregnancy? Early years?

2

What shapes family health?

Genes? Daily habits? Stress? Money?

3

What support do you actually need?

Your unique perspective helps H2H discover how fathers and co-parents shape family health, what affects YOUR own wellbeing as a parent, and what support you really need - insights that can only come from you.

Your Privacy & Rights

Multiple layers of protection for your information - click each card to learn more

🏥 Independent Oversight

Reviewed by multiple institutions

Reviewed by Lurie Children's Hospital and partner institutions. People outside the research team ensure we're protecting participants and conducting ethical research.

🛡️ Legal Protections

NIH Certificate of Confidentiality protects your information

This certificate provides federal legal protection for your information. Researchers generally cannot be forced to share information that could identify you, even by courts.
Learn more about Certificates of Confidentiality →

🔒 Data Security

Encrypted & separated storage

Your information is stored with bank-level encryption. Survey answers are kept completely separate from contact information. Our team consulted legal experts to design the strongest protections possible.

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Family Privacy

Your answers stay private

We never share your answers with the mom who invited you, and we won't share their answers with you. Everyone's information stays completely private.

✅ Your Control

You're always in charge

You can:
  • Stop participating at any time
  • Skip any questions you don't want to answer
  • Ask questions anytime
Your participation won't affect your medical care.

⚠️ Important Note

H2H is research, not medical care

Remember: H2H is research to help future families, not medical care for current health issues. Always contact your healthcare provider for medical questions or concerns about your health.

Frequently Asked Questions

How did you get my contact info?
Someone in H2H invited you - likely the mother of a child in your life. We suggest talking with them about the study.
Which child is this about?
If you have multiple children, check with the person who invited you about which child is part of H2H.
Do I have to be with the mom?
No. We want to hear from all fathers and co-parents, no matter your relationship. Your relationship status doesn't matter. Together? Apart? Single? You're still important to this research.
What if I don't see my kid much?
You still matter. Your genes (if you're the biological father) or your influence when you're there still counts.
I'm not the biological dad but I help raise this child. Do I count?
Yes! Co-parents are just as important. What you do every day might matter even more than genes.
Can I be both a biological father AND a co-parent?
Yes! You might get more than one invitation. We want to hear from you in both roles.
Is this real?
Yes! H2H is run by Lurie Children's Hospital and Northwestern University. It's paid for by the National Institutes of Health. Check out the main study page at sites.northwestern.edu/amplab/h2h
Will being in H2H help me personally?
H2H is research to help future families, not medical care. But you'll get updates on what we learn, and your voice helps create programs that could help people like you.
