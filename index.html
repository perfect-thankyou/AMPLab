What Makes H2H Groundbreaking

Research gaps we're solving and why this matters for families everywhere

H2H is the first study designed to answer questions that have puzzled researchers and doctors for decades:

Research Gap #1: How do we reach busy moms?

A New Strategy: We're the first to study heart health by connecting with moms through their young kids.

Moms with young children often lose ground on their own heart health during these busy years. They may skip their own doctor visits but never miss their kids' appointments. H2H connects with moms where they already are. What we learn could help create future programs that reach moms at their kids' doctor visits - when they're already thinking about family health.

Photo: Mom with child at pediatric clinic

Research Gap #2: Why can't we break the cycle of heart health problems?

The Missing Piece: H2H is the first to follow families from before pregnancy through early childhood to learn exactly when heart health matters most.

Scientists think mom's heart health before pregnancy affects her future health and her baby's health. But it's been hard to study this time period, since half of pregnancies aren't planned at that moment. H2H follows all families through this period, whether pregnancies are planned or not. If the time before pregnancy is that missing piece that can break the cycle - H2H will find out.

Photo: Dr. Perak with diverse pregnant patients or families

Research Gap #3: But will it actually work?

Real Families, Real Voices: H2H knows that to make programs that work for real families, we need to hear from the real experts on those families - the parents.

Including the voices of moms has not been the norm in research. Including dads and co-parents is even more rare. But while surveys and medical tests teach us a lot, complex problems like this need more. H2H directly asks moms, dads, and co-parents from diverse families what really affects their heart health and what help would actually work.

Photo: Diverse families with young children

Dr. Perak's Research Impact

  • Expert in two-generation (moms and kids/teens) cardiovascular health
  • Discovered that mom's heart health during pregnancy predicts children's heart health 13+ years later - a finding the American Heart Association named as a Top 10 Scientific Advance of the whole year
  • Research published in top medical journals, meaning the results reach scientists, public health officials, and government leaders who create policies and programs that impact families
  • Work has informed clinical guidelines - the strategies that experts agree all doctors should use to provide the best care for patients like you

How We Define Heart Health

Life's Essential 8 is the American Heart Association's science-based framework that measures heart health through diet, physical activity, sleep, avoiding nicotine, healthy weight, blood pressure, cholesterol, and blood sugar. Research shows good heart health affects not just heart disease and stroke, but also mental health, pregnancy outcomes, brain health, quality of life, and more.

Dr. Perak was the pediatric expert for the most recent Life's Essential 8 definition and the first to define it for pregnant moms and young children. Her recent systematic review catalogued hundreds of studies showing the many benefits of good heart health.

Learn more about the AMP Lab's research on Life's Essential 8 | American Heart Association Life's Essential 8

The Next Breakthrough in Family Heart Health

Prior research discovered heart health during pregnancy matters for mom and baby. That work was essential, but it won't be enough for the next breakthrough. H2H unlocks the complete journey - studying moms' heart health after prior pregnancies while raising young kids, and before any future pregnancy when heart health affects both mom and baby.

Your Role in the Discovery

Every family's story gets us closer to real solutions that protect heart health

Part A: The Overlooked Population (All 5,000 Participants)

Photo: Mom managing kids and daily life

You're helping solve the puzzle for busy moms everywhere

You're part of a rare effort to focus specifically on heart health for mothers of young children. Too often, busy moms prioritize everyone else's health while struggling with their own. You're changing that. H2H's approach of connecting with your health through your child's healthcare visits gives us real-world insights about what works for this unique life stage.

Your impact: You're helping other moms like you - ensuring they won't have to neglect their heart health or struggle alone during the demanding early parenting years.

Part B: The Missing Piece (~650 Pregnancies)

Photo: Pregnant mom with toddler

If you become pregnant, you'll unlock a second critical piece of the puzzle

We expect about 10-15% of our participants will get pregnant during the study. If you're one of them, you'll help answer a fundamental question that has puzzled scientists: WHEN is heart health most critical across generations? You'll be part of the first group to be studied from before pregnancy through toddlerhood in a diverse population.

Your impact: Your pregnancy journey fills in a unique part of the roadmap for future families. You're helping solve a puzzle that impacts every future family - your experiences, together with other H2H pregnancy journeys, create the complete path to evidence-based family programs.

What to Expect in H2H

Your journey as an H2H participant

Visual Timeline Graphic

What You'll Do

When You Join:

  • Initial Survey (about 30 minutes): Health history, current habits, family background, stress levels, future plans
  • Medical Record Permission: You give permission in the consent form; we handle all the paperwork
  • Healthcare Information: Tell us where you get healthcare so we know where to request your records

Monthly Throughout the Study:

  • Quick Check-ins (about 5 minutes): Updates on health habits, life events, contact information

If You Become Pregnant:

  • Additional Surveys: 1-4 extra 30-minute surveys over 2-3 years
  • Invite Baby's Father & Co-parent: Option to invite the biological father when pregnant and whoever helps raise the baby after birth (your choice for both)
  • Baby's Health Records: Permission for baby's medical records after birth

Optional Interviews (if invited):

  • Video Chats with Research Team: Share thoughts on heart health, ideas for programs, and experiences as a parent (40 moms and 20 dads/co-parents total will be invited)

What You'll Get

  • $25 for initial survey
  • $25-$35 for additional surveys (more after baby arrives due to extra questions)
  • $50 monthly raffle (3 winners each month for staying up-to-date)
  • $50 for each interview
  • Monthly newsletters with study updates and heart health tips
  • Knowing you're part of the solution for families like yours

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this study only for moms who want to have another baby?
Not at all! Every participant matters equally. Most of our participants may not become pregnant during the study, and their contributions are just as valuable. We're learning about heart health for ALL busy moms, plus understanding what happens during pregnancies that do occur.
How can I join H2H?
H2H enrollment is by invitation only. Moms receive invitations through their healthcare systems or other research studies they're in. Dads and co-parents are invited by moms after pregnancies occur. If you received an invitation, use your personal link to join. We're continuing to expand, so you may receive an invitation in the future.
What if I don't want to include my partner or my child's father?
That's completely your choice. We never contact anyone without your permission. Your participation won't be affected in any way if you choose not to invite others to join.
If I invite family members, do you share our answers?
No! We don't share your study answers with anyone you invite, and we don't share their answers with you or with each other. Everyone's information stays private.
How do you keep my information private?
We have multiple layers of protection: a Certificate of Confidentiality from NIH, oversight from independent institutions, secure data storage, and your survey answers kept separate from your contact information. Your name never appears in any reports. Learn more about confidentiality certificates | Read more about H2H Privacy
What if I need health help during the study?
H2H is research, not medical care. We provide resources and reminders to work with your healthcare providers for any health concerns. We can't provide medical advice, but we can help connect you with resources.
Can I quit the study if I change my mind?
Of course! You can stop participating at any time without affecting any medical care you receive. Just contact us and we'll remove you from future surveys.
Will I get my personal results?
We don't provide individual medical results - that's something to discuss with your healthcare provider. But we will definitely send you exciting updates about what H2H is learning and how it's making a real difference for families!
How long do surveys take?
The initial survey takes about 30 minutes. Monthly surveys take about 5 minutes. If you become pregnant, there would be 1-4 extra 30-minute surveys over 2-3 years, but your monthly surveys stay at about 5 minutes.
What if I move during the study?
No problem! Since H2H is completely online, you can participate from anywhere. Just update your contact information in your monthly survey.
What technology do I need?
You can use any device with internet access - smartphone, tablet, or computer. If you need help with technology or internet access, contact us and we can help you find local resources.
What if I don't have reliable internet?
Contact us! We can help you find local resources like libraries with free internet, or discuss other options for participation.
