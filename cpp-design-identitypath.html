Handling Roadblocks

Everyone faces challenges. The difference isn't avoiding roadblocks - it's planning for them!

😴 Too tired

→ Start with just 5 minutes

👥 Peer pressure

→ Have your response ready

📱 Phone distraction

→ Set specific limits

Slipped up? You can restart RIGHT NOW - mid-meal, mid-scroll, mid-anything! Don't wait for tomorrow. The next choice is yours.

Meet Alex - A Teen Just Like You

Alex is 14 years old. Their dad has diabetes, their mom has high blood pressure, and Alex sometimes worries about their own future health. Sound familiar?

Like many teens, Alex:

  • Loves gaming and social media
  • Drinks soda most days
  • Feels tired a lot
  • Doesn't love PE class
  • Wants to be healthy but isn't sure how
"I didn't want to end up sick like my parents, but I didn't know where to start..."
📍

Chapter 1: Seeing the Path

Alex learned that life is like a path with three important points:

📍
Where Alex Is Now

Age 14, some unhealthy choices, worried about following parents' health problems

"I realized quick wants were running my life - chips! soda! phone! But that's not what I truly want deep down."
🎯
Near-Term Future (This Month)

Alex practicing new choices, starting to feel different, building confidence

"I'm actually doing this! What I truly value is starting to lead more often than quick wants."
⭐
Adult Alex (The Goal)

Healthy, energetic adult, free from preventable diseases, living an active life

"I'm so glad I started practicing these choices when I was young - now it's just part of who I am!"
🎯

Chapter 2: Alex's Action Plan

Week 1 Plan:

Action 1: "Because I want to be an energetic adult, I'm someone who values energy, so I'm taking 10-minute walks right when I get home (3:15pm) Monday-Thursday"

Action 2: "Because I want to avoid diabetes, I'm practicing being a mindful eater by choosing water instead of soda at lunch"

Action 3: "Because I want to be a focused, energetic adult, I'm someone who cares about giving tomorrow-me good sleep, so I'm putting my phone in the kitchen at 9pm"

🏋️

Chapter 3: Alex's Roadblock Solutions

🌧️ "It's raining"

→ "I'll move to music in my room instead"

👥 "Friends all got soda"

→ "I'll say I'm trying to improve my game performance"

😴 "Too tired"

→ "I'll just do 5 minutes - something is better than nothing"

📱 "Worried about missing messages"

→ "I'll tell friends I'm offline after 9pm"

"At first I worried friends would think I was boring or 'health obsessed.' But then something clicked - I'm still the same fun person. I still hang out, still game, still joke around. I'm just also someone who wants to feel good. I realized I could be social AND energetic, spontaneous AND healthy."

"I did some of what I planned each day. That's way better than the nothing I was doing before! Quick wants still show up loud - my body screaming for chips, everyone else scrolling. Chapter 5: Where Alex Is After 4 Weeks

What Changed:

  • Walking is starting to feel like part of who I am (but some days are still hard)
  • Water is becoming my usual choice
  • Better sleep = more energy
  • Confidence increased ("What I truly value CAN lead instead of quick wants")
Alex's Message to You:

"I'm just a regular teen who decided to try this. I still mess up sometimes - like yesterday I watched videos till midnight. But I'm doing way better than before.

The biggest thing I learned? When something feels hard but I still want it, that means it's important to me. Quick wants still show up loud, but now I know that's not who I truly am. I'm practicing letting what I really value be in charge.

It's not about being perfect, it's just about practicing making choices from what you truly want deep down. You don't have to change everything at once. Just pick one thing and try it. If you mess up, whatever, just try again right then - don't even wait till tomorrow.

Your future self will thank you. I believe in you! 💪"
